# New Trading Ideas

Here are 5 new trading ideas that can be implemented within the existing `BaseBreakoutStrategy` framework:

### 1. Mean Reversion Strategy

*   **Concept:** This is the opposite of the breakout strategy. Instead of betting on the price continuing in the same direction, this strategy bets on the price reverting to its recent average.
*   **Implementation (`MeanReversionStrategy(BaseBreakoutStrategy)`):**
    *   Calculate the moving average (e.g., SMA or EMA) of the `price_history`.
    *   Define upper and lower bands around the moving average (e.g., using standard deviations, creating Bollinger Bands).
    *   If the `current_price` moves *above* the upper band, enter a `SHORT` trade (expecting it to fall back to the average).
    *   If the `current_price` moves *below* the lower band, enter a `LONG` trade (expecting it to rise back to the average).
*   **Why it's a good idea:** It's a classic, non-correlated strategy to the existing breakout system, which can diversify the portfolio and capture profits in ranging markets where breakout strategies typically fail.

### 2. Momentum Strategy with Volume Confirmation

*   **Concept:** A variation of the breakout strategy, but with an added layer of confirmation using trade volume (if available from the API). A breakout is only considered valid if it's accompanied by a surge in volume, indicating strong conviction behind the move.
*   **Implementation (`MomentumVolumeStrategy(BaseBreakoutStrategy)`):**
    *   The API payload for quotes would need to include a `volume` field. The `QuoteTick` data class would be updated to store it.
    *   Maintain a moving average of `volume` over the `window_size`.
    *   When a price breakout occurs (as in the current `BreakoutStrategy`), also check if the `current_volume` is significantly higher than its moving average (e.g., > 1.5x).
    *   Only `enter_trade` if both the price breakout and the volume surge conditions are met.
*   **Why it's a good idea:** This helps to filter out "false breakouts" that occur on low volume, which are more likely to reverse. It increases the probability of success for each trade taken.

### 3. "Inside the Range" Fading Strategy

*   **Concept:** This strategy is designed for choppy or sideways markets. It aims to profit from small price oscillations by trading "inside" the recent high and low.
*   **Implementation (`RangeFadeStrategy(BaseBreakoutStrategy)`):**
    *   Identify the `min_price` and `max_price` of the `price_history` window.
    *   Instead of trading when the price breaks *out*, trade when the price "fades" back from the edges of the range.
    *   If the price touches the `max_price` and then moves back down by a `pip_buffer`, enter a `SHORT` trade.
    *   If the price touches the `min_price` and then moves back up by a `pip_buffer`, enter a `LONG` trade.
*   **Why it's a good idea:** This is another strategy that performs well in market conditions where the breakout strategy struggles. It provides a way to generate income during periods of market consolidation.

### 4. RSI Overbought/Oversold Strategy

*   **Concept:** This strategy uses the Relative Strength Index (RSI), a popular momentum indicator, to identify overbought and oversold conditions.
*   **Implementation (`RSIStrategy(BaseBreakoutStrategy)`):**
    *   You would need to add a function to calculate the RSI based on the `price_history`. This is a standard formula that can be easily implemented.
    *   Define RSI thresholds for overbought (e.g., 70) and oversold (e.g., 30) in `config.json`.
    *   When the RSI crosses *above* the overbought threshold (e.g., 70), enter a `SHORT` trade.
    *   When the RSI crosses *below* the oversold threshold (e.g., 30), enter a `LONG` trade.
*   **Why it's a good idea:** The RSI is a well-established and powerful indicator. It can be more effective than pure price-based levels for identifying turning points, especially when combined with the existing multi-window and parameter optimization framework.

### 5. Dynamic Stop-Loss and Take-Profit based on Volatility

*   **Concept:** Instead of using fixed `tp_pips` and `sl_pips` from the config, this idea makes them dynamic based on recent market volatility. When the market is volatile, use wider stops and targets; when it's calm, use tighter ones.
*   **Implementation (Modification to `BaseBreakoutStrategy`):**
    *   In the `BaseBreakoutStrategy`, before entering a trade, calculate a measure of volatility from the `price_history`. The Average True Range (ATR) is a standard indicator for this, but even a simple standard deviation would work.
    *   Define a multiplier in `config.json` (e.g., `atr_tp_multiplier: 2.0`, `atr_sl_multiplier: 1.0`).
    *   When calling `self.enter_trade`, dynamically calculate the `tp_pips` and `sl_pips` for that specific trade based on the current volatility. For example: `tp_pips = current_atr * atr_tp_multiplier`.
    *   The `enter_trade` method and the `open_trade` data structure would need to be modified to store these dynamic values for each trade.
*   **Why it's a good idea:** This adapts the strategy to changing market conditions. Fixed stop-losses can get hit too easily in volatile markets, and fixed take-profits can leave money on the table. Dynamic stops and targets improve the risk-reward profile of any entry logic.
