## Positioning

### Product Name
- Trading Strategy Warehouse

### Core Offer
- A strategy-centric product that gives users a structured, recurring way to discover, review, and stay connected to trading strategy ideas and warehouse updates.

### Market Promise
- "Follow one place that continuously surfaces strategy-driven trading ideas and weekly strategy warehouse updates without needing to search the market manually."

### Why Someone Subscribes
- They want a steady pipeline of strategy-focused trading content.
- They want to keep up with warehouse additions, highlights, or curated strategy insights.
- They prefer an organized source rather than fragmented social posts alone.

## Audience Definition

### Primary Audience
- independent retail traders
- strategy experimenters
- traders already consuming market or trading content online
- users who respond to structured ideas, comparisons, rankings, or weekly curated updates

### Secondary Audience
- social followers interested in trading systems but not yet committed to a subscription
- newsletter consumers willing to subscribe for regular strategy updates
- technically curious users attracted by warehouse depth, consistency, and ongoing discovery

### Audience Pain Points
- too much noisy trading content
- not enough structured strategy discovery
- difficulty finding a dependable stream of strategy-oriented material
- weak continuity between one-off posts and a long-term learning or following habit

## Messaging Framework

### Headline Direction
- "Discover trading strategies worth following, every week."

### Supporting Message
- "Trading Strategy Warehouse gives you a consistent stream of structured strategy ideas, highlights, and updates so you can follow strategy evolution instead of chasing random noise."

### Core Messaging Themes
- structure over noise
- recurring discovery
- strategy-first insight
- weekly reasons to return
- one destination for ongoing warehouse value

## Funnel Promise

### Awareness Layer
- Social content should aggressively increase reach using concise, high-frequency, strategy-led hooks.
- Example content angles:
  - strategy comparisons
  - "what changed this week"
  - ranked ideas
  - warehouse highlights
  - short educational breakdowns

### Conversion Layer
- Landing page should convert traffic into a simple subscription action.
- Primary CTA:
  - subscribe for weekly strategy warehouse updates

### Retention Layer
- Subscribers should receive recurring weekly value, not just a one-time signup outcome.
- Retention should be driven by:
  - weekly warehouse update summaries
  - featured strategy highlights
  - progress or insight narratives

## CTA Path

### Primary CTA
- "Subscribe for weekly strategy warehouse updates"

### CTA Flow
1. social post or campaign content creates attention
2. user clicks through to the landing page
3. user sees the product promise, value summary, and weekly benefit
4. user subscribes
5. user enters a weekly retention or nurture flow
