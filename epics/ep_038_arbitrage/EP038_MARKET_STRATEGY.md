# EP038 — Market Strategy for Live Spread Intelligence

Created: 2026-06-27

## Product to market

EP038 is a website publishing continuously refreshed pricing spreads as live digital product entries.

Positioning:

```text
Live marketplace spread intelligence for resellers and opportunity hunters.
```

The customer is not buying a product to resell.

The customer is buying:

```text
time saved + spread discovery + source memory + price-spike awareness + risk filtering
```

## Core marketing thesis

People already search manually for arbitrage opportunities across eBay, outlets, domains, clearance pages, marketplaces, and supplier sites.

EP038 markets itself as:

```text
the live board that does the searching, scoring, and monitoring for them
```

## Primary customer segments

### 1. Side-hustle marketplace sellers

People who sell on eBay/Vinted/Depop/Facebook Marketplace/Amazon Marketplace and want leads.

Pain:

- spend hours searching;
- hard to know what has real spread;
- prices move quickly;
- do not know good source sites.

Message:

```text
Stop hunting blind. Watch active product spreads in one place.
```

### 2. Sneaker/trainer resellers

Pain:

- sizes/variants matter;
- price spikes happen;
- outlet/retail/source stock changes;
- authenticity risk.

Message:

```text
Monitor trainer price gaps before they close.
```

### 3. Domain investors

Pain:

- hard to find undervalued expired/closeout domains;
- need comparable sales and trend signals;
- many bad domains waste time.

Message:

```text
A filtered watchlist of domain spread candidates, not raw expired-domain noise.
```

### 4. Digital product sellers

Pain:

- resale rights are confusing;
- platform rules are risky;
- many apparent spreads are non-compliant.

Message:

```text
Only digital-product spread candidates with rights and policy risk notes.
```

## Initial wedge

Do not market every category at once.

Start with one wedge:

```text
Live Spread Board for UK marketplace resellers
```

Then split into category feeds once demand is visible:

- trainers;
- domains;
- clearance products;
- digital products with resale rights;
- local marketplace flips.

## Proof-first marketing plan

### Phase 1 — Manual proof board

Goal:

```text
prove users understand and want the board before automation
```

Actions:

1. Publish a simple page with 10 sample spread cards.
2. Make timestamps/freshness visible.
3. Show risk notes so it feels credible, not scammy.
4. Gate the full source links behind early-access request or email.
5. Ask users what categories they want monitored.

### Phase 2 — Audience discovery

Post in/around communities where arbitrage/reselling conversations already happen.

Channels:

- X/Twitter reseller/search threads;
- Reddit communities where allowed, without spam;
- Facebook reseller groups where allowed;
- eBay seller communities/forums;
- domain investor communities;
- side-hustle newsletters;
- local WhatsApp/Telegram reseller networks if available;
- YouTube/TikTok short demos showing before/after spread discovery.

Important: avoid spam. Lead with sample value, not hype.

### Phase 3 — Content engine

Create recurring content:

```text
Spread of the Day
3 spreads that closed this week
What killed this spread?
Source price dropped, marketplace price held
Trainer spread watch
Domain spread watch
```

Each post routes to:

```text
live board + waitlist
```

### Phase 4 — Paid access test

Before building full automation, test willingness to pay:

- £5–£9 early-access monthly for limited board;
- £19–£29/month for refreshed niche board;
- one-off £9 category report;
- founder access for first 20 users.

Do not overprice until data quality is proven.

## Differentiation

Most arbitrage content is vague:

```text
"buy low, sell high"
```

EP038 differentiates by showing:

- specific spread cards;
- timestamps;
- source/marketplace comparison;
- confidence score;
- risk notes;
- history of whether spreads persisted or expired.

## Trust strategy

The site must not look like a get-rich-quick scheme.

Trust builders:

- show expired spreads as expired;
- show bad/risky candidates as rejected;
- explain fees/returns/policy risk;
- never guarantee profit;
- publish methodology;
- show last checked timestamp;
- show confidence levels honestly.

## First 14-day launch plan

### Days 1–2

- Build static sample board.
- Add 10 manually researched sample spreads.
- Add email/waitlist capture.

### Days 3–5

- Create 5 short posts showing example spread cards.
- Create one explainer thread: "What is a pricing spread?"
- Ask for category votes.

### Days 6–10

- Share in relevant places carefully.
- DM only people already known/consenting; no cold spam.
- Collect objections/questions.
- Update board daily or every other day.

### Days 11–14

- Publish "what changed" update:
  - spreads that stayed live;
  - spreads that expired;
  - biggest observed spread;
  - categories with strongest signal.
- Offer early-access founder list.

## Messaging guardrails

Use:

```text
research leads
pricing-gap intelligence
spread candidates
live watchlist
verify before acting
```

Avoid:

```text
guaranteed profit
risk-free
make £X per day
buy this and earn
```

## Best first market angle

Recommended first angle:

```text
For UK marketplace resellers: a live board of pricing spreads worth checking today.
```

This is broad enough to test demand but specific enough that users understand it.

## Success metrics

First validation targets:

- 50 landing-page visits;
- 10 waitlist signups;
- 3 conversations with potential users;
- 1 person willing to pay for early access;
- repeated request for a specific category feed.

If those happen, build automation.

If not, adjust category, positioning, or source quality before building further.
